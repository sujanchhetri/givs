About GIVS Nepal

Guest Intern Volunteer Service offers a wide range of Internship and Volunteer programs for international Medical students Nepal. We cut out unnecessary costs to provide our students the most affordable and best experience of program/project. Unlike other organizations, the program criteria mainly depend on the interest/schedule of the participant. Participant (Internship program) needs to submit the details regarding his/her program of interest/curriculum at least two months before the program date along with a letter of confirmation emailed from the university to the organization.

Internship programs are planned for University students, graduates and career developers who want to explore their interests and develop professional skills and competencies. Furthermore, Volunteer programs are generally considered as a selfless activity/service who donates his/her time and effort to help the people in need. As mentioned above, volunteers are able to work on their chosen field of interest. They require to submit a brief report of their interest on the respected field of work at least two months before.

Our services are targeted at providing quality work in a safe environment. On of our staffs will be always attentive to the needs of the interns/volunteers throughout their session with GIVS in Nepal.

A sense of justice and a sense of fairness are the two main factors to which our organization adheres to.

CAUSE OF EVOLUTION

In the area of child health, providing vitamin A supplementation, immunizations, family planning counseling, safe motherhood interventions, community-based integrated management of childhood illnesses, particularly the detection and treatment of pneumonia and diarrhea—the top two causes of child deaths in Nepal— and community-based newborn care package is the essential health services that need to be expanded in all corners of the country.The biannual vitamin A supplementation helps to prevent an estimated 12,000 child deaths per year. There are numerous challenges that country still needs to deal with.

  • The neonatal mortality rate (rate of death during first 28 days after birth) remains high as 21 deaths per 1000 live births, whereas under-five mortality is 39 deaths per 1000 live births.
  • The neonatal mortality rate is twice as high among the poorest wealth quintile compared with the richest one, and higher among younger mothers.

Improving the health of the children in Nepal is the core objective of GIVS and we strongly aims to strive on scaling up sustainable, integrated, community-focused interventions to deliver quality of services to the children of Nepal from marginalized communities, underprivileged and disabled. GIVS have come up with a commitment to serve the less fortunate children by contributing 20% of the organization’s fund. Furthermore, GIVS predominantly works on these health sectors.

  • Vaccine Coverage
  • Growth monitoring Visits
  • Nutrition supplement distribution
  • Treatment of illness
